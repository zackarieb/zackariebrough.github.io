This web page shall explain how the Power Supply powers a computer, and everything else behind the scenes of the power system.

Power Supply

At its core, the power supply converts high voltage AC energy to low voltage DC energy.

Power supplys have a few different variants. These variants allow a computer to run/operate better depending upon the situation

Power supplies are often sorted by how much voltage they provide, such as the list below.

Some of the differnet variants of power supplies would be

  • 3.3 volts
  • 5 volts
  • 12 volts

The volts used to organize computers are based off of the amount of voltage in their rails. Power supplies contain numerous rails, with each rail containing a different amount of voltage.
